Game Instructions: House of Jokers

Players: 3–5
Equipment: A standard deck (52 cards) and 3 jokers.

1. Setup

  • The Start: Take out all four sevens and place them in a row in the middle of the board.
  • Deal: Set the jokers aside. Deal all remaining cards evenly to the players. This is your hidden hand.
  • The Blind Choice: Each player selects three cards from their hand and places them face-down. These three slots are your playable cards.
  • The Reveal: On a given signal, everyone reveals their three cards simultaneously.
  • Start: The player with the most cards in hand starts.

2. Communication

  • You may only talk about the cards visible on the board or among the playable cards.
  • It is strictly forbidden to reveal or hint at what you have left in your hidden hand.

3. Objective

  • Win: All 52 cards are placed in complete straight flushes (Ace to King).
  • You lose immediately if a player who already has three face-down cards fails a move or guesses wrong (see the Mercy Rule).

4. How to Play

Play proceeds clockwise. If you have a valid move (a playable card that fits on a straight and is not blocked), you must act. You then choose between two options:

Option A: Play a card

  • Play one of your playable cards to the correct spot on a straight.
  • Lock the straight: If you play an Ace or a King, immediately turn that pile face down. That side is now closed.
  • Refill: Take a new card from your hand to fill the empty spot. Did you play a face-up card? Refill with a face-up card. Did you play a face-down card? Refill with a face-down card.

Option B: Handle a Joker (Move or Remove)

  • If you have a card (face-up or face-down) that could be played on a straight, you can choose to move a joker instead.
  • The Proof:
    Face-up card: Point to the playable card in front of you.
    Face-down card: State what the card is, show it to everyone, and return it face-down.
  • Move: Move any joker to a new open endpoint.
  • Remove: If a suit is completely finished (both Ace and King are played), you may use your turn to remove a joker entirely. Place it on the seven of that suit.
  • No refill: Since you didn't play a card to the board, do not draw a new one.

5. If you CANNOT act

If none of your cards fit, or if your paths are entirely blocked by jokers:

  • Penalty: You must choose one of your face-up playable cards and turn it face-down.
  • The Mercy Rule: If you already have three face-down cards in front of you, you are 'at mercy'. If it becomes your turn again and you still can't move, or if you try to 'prove' a face-down card but guess wrong – the game is immediately lost for the whole group.

6. Laws of the Jokers

  • Block: No card can be played where a joker lies.
  • Start: The first three cards played after the sevens get a joker placed on them immediately.
  • Placement: Max one joker per endpoint.
  • Forbidden zones: A joker may never be placed on a seven (until removed permanently) or on a closed side.

7. Memory and Endgame

  • Memory Error: If you guess wrong on a face-down card (during proof or play), the card remains face-down. As a penalty, you must turn down an additional face-up card.
  • Empty hand: When your hidden hand is empty, you play with your remaining playable cards. No new cards are drawn.
  • Finished player: When a player has played all their cards, they are completely done and no longer participate in the round. The remaining players continue to play in clockwise order.
